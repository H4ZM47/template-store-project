Introduction

This Data Classification Standards Document aims to set forth the standards and protocols for data handling and classification for {{COMPANY_NAME}}. The document outlines the roles and responsibilities of each department, data classification categories, labeling, and storage/transmission requirements.

Objectives

  1. To protect sensitive information from unauthorized access and data breaches.
  2. To comply with regulatory requirements, including GDPR, CCPA, and other industry standards.
  3. To ensure all team members understand their roles in data protection.
  4. To set clear guidelines for data storage, transmission, and handling.

Scope

This document applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties who have access to, or are responsible for, the company's data assets.

Definitions

  • Data: Information that is collected, used, or stored digitally.
  • Classification: The process of categorizing data based on its sensitivity level.
  • Data Owner: The person responsible for the data's creation, development, and integrity.
  • Data Custodian: The person responsible for the safe storage, retrieval, and maintenance of the data.

Roles and Responsibilities

Executive

  • Approve and enforce data classification policies.
  • Allocate resources for data classification and security.

Engineering

  • Implement security features that protect classified data.
  • Regularly update the SaaS application to comply with the latest security standards.

Product

  • Ensure that data classification is part of the product development lifecycle.
  • Work with Engineering to prioritize security features.

Sales

  • Responsible for securing customer data during the sales process.
  • Ensure that no sensitive data is shared without proper classification and approval.

Customer Success

  • Handle customer data according to its classification.
  • Communicate security features and policies to customers.

Marketing

  • Ensure any customer data used for marketing purposes is properly classified and protected.
  • Comply with privacy regulations when gathering user data.

Finance

  • Responsible for classifying and securing all financial records.
  • Ensure that financial data is only accessible to authorized individuals.

HR

  • Keep all employee records and sensitive personal information secure.
  • Ensure new employees are trained on data classification and security.

Data Classification Categories

Company Confidential

  • Trade secrets, product roadmaps, and proprietary algorithms.
  • Access restricted to authorized personnel only.

Company Financial

  • Quarterly reports, investor relations communications, and internal financial data.
  • Access restricted to Finance and Executive teams.

Company Public

  • Publicly available information such as product descriptions, blog posts, and press releases.

Personally Identifiable Information

  • Customer and employee data that could be used to identify an individual.
  • Protected under various legal frameworks.

Criminal Justice Information

  • Data related to legal matters or proceedings.
  • Highly restricted and must comply with legal standards for storage and sharing.

Classification Process

  1. Data Identification
  2. Data Categorization
  3. Data Labeling
  4. Data Handling and Storage

Data Labeling

All data should be labeled according to its classification and handled accordingly.

Storage and Transmission Requirements

  • Company Confidential and Financial Data: Stored in encrypted databases, transmitted over secure channels.
  • PII and Criminal Justice Information: Stored in compliance with legal requirements, including encryption and multi-factor authentication.

Training and Awareness

  • All employees must undergo data classification upon hire and annually thereafter.
  • Data classification training will be incorporated into the overall security training program.

Policy Violations

Violations of this policy can result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

Compliance and Auditing

Internal and external audits will be conducted periodically to ensure compliance with this policy.

Continuous Improvement

This policy will be reviewed at least annually or as needed to adapt to new regulatory or business requirements.
